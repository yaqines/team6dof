AI and Autonomy

From pixels to decisions.

Perception is designed to support the actual scoring tasks, not just produce detections. The pipeline combines onboard imagery, filtering, localization, and mission logic so that detections can translate into usable mapping outputs and delivery decisions.

01
Image Capture
A mission camera captures frames over the search boundary and mission area.
02
Preprocessing
Frames are prepared for inference and tagged with pose data for downstream mapping and localization.
03
Detection
Targets of interest are detected and filtered to reduce false positives before any action is taken.
04
Localization
Pixel-space detections are converted into usable world positions using altitude, heading, and camera geometry.
05
Decision Output
Targets are passed to mission logic for review, re-survey, mapping output, or payload delivery planning.
Detection Logic

Confidence before action

Detections are filtered before they become mission actions. The system favors confidence, repeat observations, and operator awareness over risky one-shot decisions.

That lets the team preserve reliability while still making use of AI for mission scoring.

Mapping Output

Maps that are actually useful

Risk Mapping is treated as a real deliverable, with emphasis on coverage, image quality, and stitching that can hold up under competition judging.

The same perception stack also supports delivery target identification and localization.

Mission Strategy

Reliable first.
Then ambitious.

The strategy is to align design effort with what the team can actually test, integrate, and repeat under competition conditions. Complexity is added only when it survives contact with integration and field testing.

Waypoint Endurance
Autonomous laps matter, so mission planning starts with flight performance, navigation quality, and reliable autonomous execution before entering the search boundary.
Search, Detect, Deliver
Perception, localization, and payload delivery are designed to work as one chain rather than as isolated features.
Operator Efficiency
The mission structure aims to reduce unnecessary operator load while preserving safe oversight and strong field awareness.
What we prioritize
Clean subsystem ownership with integration discipline
Repeatable mission flow before edge-case complexity
Testing paths that expose real failures early
Documentation that reflects the actual build
Operator clarity during mission execution
Trade-offs we accept
Conservative autonomy decisions over fragile cleverness
Subsystem changes must justify integration cost
Testing time competes directly with added complexity

Testing and Validation

We test until it is predictable.

Testing is staged from subsystem validation to integrated rehearsal. The goal is not just to prove capability once, but to remove uncertainty before competition.

01
Subsystem Testing
Payload, avionics, GN&C, AI, and GCS functions are validated independently before integration.
02
Simulation
Mission logic and autonomy behavior are checked in simulation before being trusted in the field.
03
Bench Integration
All major interfaces are checked on the bench first: power, telemetry, data exchange, and trigger paths.
04
Flight Envelope Expansion
Manual flight, hover, autonomous pathing, and mission behaviors are expanded in a controlled order.
05
Mission Rehearsal
The full mission flow is practiced under timing pressure with the actual operator structure and GCS layout.
06
Documentation Feedback Loop
Design choices, procedures, and website content are revised to reflect the build as it actually matures.
